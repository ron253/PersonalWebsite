About

The Gender Equity project is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals(SDG) of the Union’s 2030 agenda to combat gender discrimination and promote inclusiveness. As part of the Los Angeles city-wide effort to align plans to the SDG’s goals, I was assigned to research on the topic of Gender Equity and make recommendations on how City websites can become more inclusive through the use of language and images.

Problem: Lack of Gender-neutral pronouns and images discriminates persons of LGBTQIA+ and minorities and creates gender stereotypes.

By incorporating Gender-neutral pronouns(Ex.they/them/their), this creates inclusiveness by raising awareness of non-binary, LGBTQIA+ person or persons that do not self-identify themselves as binary(Ex.he/she). Gender-neutral pronouns also eliminates gender-biases that is commonly used in the English language when referencing an individual(he/she).

Process: Developed a web-scraping script to extract and analyze the type of pronouns being used across City websites.

I approached the task firsthand by manually reading and reviewing the pronouns being used across different City web pages. This approach was inefficient because I made a goal to review all 43 City websites for its language and images during the tenure of internship. And manually doing this would not be feasible. So, I thought of an innovative approach to expedite the review of pronouns by discovering web-scraping; a tool used for extracting data from web pages. With no previous programming experience besides having only taken a Java class in high school, I gave web-scraping a try and decided to develop a web-scraper in Javascript in which I failed. I wasn’t discouraged at all because this failure just made me motivated to learn more. I decided to switch to Python since it was a beginner-friendly language and started to use a variety of resources like Codecademy and youtube videos. The persistence to learn and get the job done allowed me to successfully develop a web-scraping script that extracted over 100+ non-gender inclusive pronouns from City websites, reviewed its context, then made the appropriate recommendations for replacing the non-gender-inclusive pronouns.

Outcome: Determined the number and types of non-gender inclusive pronouns used across City websites

Developed a pie chart and histogram to show the percentages of gender-inclusive vs non-gender inclusive City websites calculated by the aggregate non-gender inclusive pronouns. Then, I presented to the Gender Equity and web development team on the data analysis and recommendations on how future City websites can structure their websites to be more inclusive through the use of gender-neutral pronouns and images.
